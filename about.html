Who are we ?

The University Innovation Pod (UniPod), is a unique initiative located in a top technical Public University with the objective of supporting youth innovation. It is a physical space for collaboration among students, faculty, and community members at the University of Juba. It aims to enhance innovation and accelerate learning by connecting university research with commercialization. UniPod offers equipment and programs to enhance technology transfer, Intellectual Property Registration, and Royalty programs. Furthermore, students will learn how to design and construct prototypes, digital fabrication, and interaction, allowing the creation of products that are feasible from a business standpoint.

// Our Passion //

Creating solutions and products ready for market, based on collaborative innovative projects developed by faculty personnel, students, diaspora South Sudanese and youth. Backed with a framework of collaborative / sustainable partnerships locally and internationally that spur innovation to thrive within a public university setting that creates solutions and products for the needs first of South Sudanese, Africans, and the globe at large.

Mission, Vision, Values

The strategic planning process reaffirms the mission and vision of the community, the strategic priorities to accomplish the vision, and reconfirms values to maintain as the UNIPOD works towards meeting the priorities.

Mission

To provide a sustainable and effective UNIPOD environment that strives on innovation, creativity for faculty, students and the youthful communities of South Sudan where digital technologies will be a critical enabler.

UNIPOD PROGRAMMES

The UniPod programs are grouped in three;

1-Innovation of Products and Solutions ready for Market, (Comprising six core programs of the UniPod)

POD TOURS

Aimed at exposing a large number of students to what the UNIPOD does in order to inspire them to engage with the UNIPOD and to aspire to becoming and Innovator

Engineering Interns Program (EIP)

Aimed at deepening and broadening the digital fabircation understanding and practical experience of engineering students who would receive training, work on their own projects, and work as tech support assisting other users of the Marketspace+.

Faculty-Student Projects

Availing the Marketspace+ facilities to faculty who apply to work on specific student projects during the academic year,

Product Showcase

In addition to weekly Product Demos, an annual flagship event with wide visibility would be organized to showcase the prototypes developed through the above programs.

Designers-Innovators Program (DIP)

DIP is aimed at students outside of engineering to design and produce prototypes in the UNIPOD through structured training on both Design Thinking, as well as Digital Fabrication, and practical experience working in the Makerspace+.

Non-University Programs (NUP)

when the University is not in session, special programs would be offered to individuals outside of the university, either from other universities or the general public, to go through an adapted intensive version of the DIP in a shorter time period. These programs would be on a fee basis, to enable the UNIPOD to cover its costs.
